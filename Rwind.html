Wind Energy Systems in Tamil Nadu

Onshore Wind farms

Wind turbine that's located on land and generates electricity from the wind.

  • Often found in rural areas or fields
  • Muppandal Wind farm installed capacity is 1,500 MW.
  • 4th largest wind farm in the world.

Small Wind Turbines

wind turbines that generate electricity for small-scale use. These turbines are typically smaller than those found in wind farms.

  • Generally used for small scale usage for homes,small factories.
  • Power produced is in the range of 55 KW to 600 KW.

Tamilnadu's Muppandal Wind Farm

Asia's Largest Onshore Wind Farm

Located in Kanyakumari district, the Muppandal Wind Farm is Tamil Nadu's flagship renewable energy project with:

  • 1,500 MW Total Installed Capacity
  • 3,000+ Wind Turbines Operational
  • 40% of TN's Wind Energy Production
  • 4,000+ Hectares Coverage Area

The farm utilizes advanced 3.4 MW turbines with 150m rotor diameter, capable of generating electricity even at low wind speeds. It powers over 1.2 million homes annually and offsets 3.5 million tons of CO₂ emissions.

Recent Wind Energy Updates (2024-2025)

Policy Update

05 Dec 2024

Refurbishment and Life extension policy

state recently released the Tamil Nadu Repowering, Refurbishment and Life Extension Policy for Wind Power Projects, 2024, valid till March 31, 2030. The policy also takes into account life extension projects that focus on extending the operational life of turbines beyond their design life or the typical 20-year lifespan.

Modernization

05 Dec 2024

Optimising old small wind turbine projects

Currently, turbines with capacities of up to 10 MW are available in the domestic and international markets. By upgrading to these new turbines, the wind energy potential of each site can be optimised, leading to increased installed capacity and generation.

Micro wind turbine to come up at Panjapur

Partnership

Hyundai Motors India Limited

Hyundai Motor India Limited (HMIL) will be setting up two renewable energy plants in Tamil Nadu for ₹38 crore as part of its target of transitioning to using 100% renewable energy by 2025.

Innovation

2024

Offsetters Community

This project involves the implementation of an 8.5 MW capacity wind power project which consists of 13 Wind Turbine Generators (WTGs) near villages in Kanyakumari, Tirunelveli and Coimbatore, in Tamil Nadu. The project converts wind energy to electrical energy. Power generated by the wind turbines will be both exported to the grid and captured for storage.
